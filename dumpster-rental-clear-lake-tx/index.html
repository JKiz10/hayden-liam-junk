Worth Knowing

Which City Are You Actually In?

Here is the thing most people never think about until it matters: Clear Lake is not one city. Clear Lake City was annexed into the City of Houston in 1977, so if your address is off Bay Area Boulevard or El Camino Real, you live in Houston and Houston's rules apply to your street.

Drive five minutes and that changes completely. Nassau Bay, El Lago, Taylor Lake Village, Seabrook, and Webster are each their own incorporated city, each with its own city hall and its own code. Kemah is not even in the same county as most of them: it sits in Galveston County while those five are in Harris.

Why it matters for a dumpster: your driveway is the simple answer everywhere. On your own concrete, you are dealing with your HOA or deed restrictions, not city permitting. The moment the container goes in the street, it is governed by whichever city you are actually standing in. Houston, for one, requires a permit for anything obstructing a roadway or sidewalk, handled by Houston Public Works at 832-395-3020. The smaller cities each set their own rules.

Tell us your address and we will tell you straight what we know. And if you want it in the street rather than the driveway, we will check with your city on the permit before your drop-off day, so it is one less thing on your plate.
