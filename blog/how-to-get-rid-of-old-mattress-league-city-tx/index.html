How to Get Rid of an Old Mattress in League City, TX

Published June 30, 2026 · Updated July 3, 2026

Hi, if you have an old mattress to get rid of in League City, the short answer is this: you cannot just drag it to the curb and expect it to disappear. Mattresses are excluded from a lot of standard municipal pickup, so your real options are donation, recycling, or a haul-off service that takes the whole set for you. The easiest path for most people is a haul-off, and that is exactly what we do.

H&L Junk Removal is local to League City, we are fully insured, and we can grab your mattress, box spring, and frame in one stop. Below is the honest breakdown of every option so you can pick what works for you.

Why you cannot just put a mattress on the curb

A mattress looks like normal bulk trash, but it does not get treated like normal bulk trash. Here is what usually goes wrong when people set one out at the curb in League City:

  • It gets left behind. Mattresses are often excluded from regular and heavy-trash collection, so the truck rolls past it and leaves it sitting on your grass for days.
  • It can turn into a code issue. A mattress sitting curbside for a week is the kind of thing a neighbor or an HOA notices, and it can put you on the wrong side of a property rule.
  • Weather ruins it fast. One good Gulf Coast rain and that mattress is soaked, heavy, and impossible to donate or recycle. Now you have a bigger problem than you started with.

Before you drag anything to the curb, it is worth confirming what your specific collection service will and will not take. League City residential heavy trash runs through AmeriWaste, and their rules on mattresses and box springs are not always what people assume. A quick check saves you from a mattress marinating on your lawn.

Donation: great when the mattress is genuinely still good

If your mattress is clean, structurally sound, and something you would actually let a guest sleep on, donation is a solid first choice. It keeps a usable item out of the landfill and helps a family nearby.

A few honest guardrails before you go this route:

  • Most charities will not accept a mattress with stains, tears, sagging, or any sign of bed bugs. Their standards are strict, and that is a good thing.
  • Call ahead. Acceptance policies change, and a wasted trip with a mattress strapped to your roof is nobody's idea of a good afternoon.
  • Some places will pick up, some will not. If you cannot haul it yourself and they do not offer pickup, you are back to needing help moving it.

Donation is the right answer for a small share of mattresses. For the older, worn-out ones, keep reading.

Recycling: better for the planet, harder to arrange

A surprising amount of a mattress can be recycled. The steel springs, the foam, the fibers, and the wood frame can all be broken down and reused instead of buried. If keeping material out of the landfill matters to you, recycling is the responsible move.

The catch is logistics. Dedicated mattress recycling is not on every corner in Galveston County, drop-off can involve a fee, and you still have to physically get a heavy, awkward mattress and box spring into a vehicle and out to the facility. For a lot of folks, that is the part that never happens, and the mattress ends up living in the garage for another year.

Good news: when we haul a mattress, we route it responsibly whenever we can, so you get the environmental upside without doing the heavy lifting or the driving.
