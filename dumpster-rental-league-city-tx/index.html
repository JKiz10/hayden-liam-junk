Worth Knowing

Why Not Just Haul It Yourself?

Fair question, and for a small job you probably should. But it is worth knowing exactly what the cheap options here will and will not do before you spend a Saturday finding out.

AmeriWaste, the city's residential hauler, does let League City residents drop construction debris at its Highway 6 facility. The catch is in the fine print: once a month, proof of residency required (driver's license and a recent water bill), and the load is capped at roughly the truck-bed height of a standard pickup. Genuinely useful for a closet's worth of demo. It is not going to move a bathroom, a roof, or a garage.

Past that, you are driving to a landfill on somebody else's hours. The nearest site taking construction and demolition debris is Dixie Farm Road Landfill up in Pearland. The nearest ones that also take household waste are Coastal Plains in Alvin and the Galveston County Landfill in Santa Fe. Every one of them means loading the truck, driving out, paying at the gate, and then doing the whole thing again. And again.

A roll-off is one trip, and it is ours. The can sits in your driveway, you fill it whenever you actually get to it, and we haul it to the right facility. That is the entire pitch.
