Worth Knowing

Ditches, Not Curbs

Dickinson is not laid out like a newer subdivision, and that genuinely changes where a roll-off can sit. The city's drainage criteria are direct about it: streets built without curb and gutter drain into roadside open ditches. If your place fronts one of those, there is no curb to set against, and a soft ditch shoulder is not somewhere you want several tons of loaded container.

None of that is a problem, it just means we should talk first. A firm driveway or a solid pad is almost always the answer out here. Tell us what your frontage looks like when you call, or text a photo to 832-425-7749. We would much rather sort that out before the trailer is in your street than after.

Still Rebuilding

We Know What Harvey Did Here

The National Weather Service gauge at Dickinson recorded 43.92 inches of rain from Hurricane Harvey in August 2017. The city's own buyout paperwork puts it plainer than we could: 13-foot flood water that "damaged or destructed nearly 80% of residential structures," more than 2,000 homes, with 10,730 households filing for FEMA assistance afterward.

There is a reason it hits Dickinson so hard. Dickinson Bayou drains Alvin, Friendswood, League City, Santa Fe, and the unincorporated county across its 22 miles to Galveston Bay, and roughly 55% of that watershed sits in the 100-year floodplain. The city now requires new and substantially improved structures to be built 18 inches above base flood elevation, which is stricter than the federal minimum.

That history is still in a lot of walls around here. If you are elevating, gutting, or finally redoing the room that got wet, a roll-off you fill over a week is the right tool for it. Wet drywall, flooring, insulation, cabinets, and framing all go straight in.
